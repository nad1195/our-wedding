How a Korean Wedding Works

Korean weddings follow a clear and organized process. Here is each step, so you know exactly what we are preparing and what to expect.

Step 1

Book the Wedding Hall (예식장 예약)

In Korea, wedding venues — called 예식장 (yeshikjang) — are booked 12 to 18 months in advance. Couples visit several halls and choose based on style, size, and date. The venue takes care of most logistics on the wedding day itself.

Step 2 · Very important

Sanggyeonrye — First Family Meeting (상견례)

Before the wedding, both families meet formally for the first time over dinner. This is one of the most meaningful Korean pre-wedding traditions — and one we have been thinking about carefully. We explain more in the next section.

Step 3

Wedding Photo Shoot (스튜디오 촬영)

Korean couples do a professional photo shoot a few months before the wedding — in a studio and often outdoors too. These photos are displayed at the venue on the wedding day and kept as a lifelong memory.

Step 4

Wedding Dress & Hanbok Fitting

The bride wears a white dress for the ceremony, then changes into a Hanbok — a beautiful traditional Korean outfit — for the Paebaek rite afterward. Most brides rent these from a bridal shop.

Step 5 · The Big Day

The Ceremony & Paebaek (폐백)

The ceremony lasts about 30–40 minutes. Vows are exchanged, rings are given, and guests celebrate at a reception banquet. Afterward comes the Paebaek — a private family rite where the couple in Hanbok bows to the groom's family, who toss dates and chestnuts as a blessing for happiness and children.

The Family Meeting — Sanggyeonrye

This is one of the most important pre-wedding moments, and one we want to be honest and open with you about.

What is Sanggyeonrye?

상견례 — Both Families Meet for the First Time

Sanggyeonrye is a formal dinner where the groom's family and bride's family meet for the first time. It is warm and respectful — a moment to greet each other, share a meal, and talk about the wedding together. In Korean culture, this happens before the wedding preparations begin in earnest.

Why we're hoping to hold it in Seoul

We have thought about this carefully, and we want to share our honest reasoning with you. We love you and we want to do this right — so here is the reality of who would need to travel if we held the Sanggyeonrye in Indonesia:

People who would need to fly to Indonesia
Nadya & Jin
Both working full-time, need to take time off
Jin's parents
Both working, need to take time off
Jin's sister & husband
Both working, need to take time off
Nadya's sibling
Also working, would need to join

That is seven people taking international flights, booking accommodation, and taking days off work — all at a significant cost. For everyone travelling from Korea, this would be a very difficult and expensive trip.

We understand that in Indonesian tradition, the groom's family comes to the bride — and we respect that deeply. But we also hope you can understand that in this situation, what is traditional and what is possible are pulling in different directions. We are not trying to dismiss tradition. We are trying to find a way that is loving, realistic, and full of meaning for everyone.

When you visit Seoul for the Sanggyeonrye, we also want to share our everyday life with you. Things like going apartment hunting together, accompanying Nadya to look at wedding dresses, and experiencing a Hanbok fitting — these are the quiet, real moments we want you to be part of. Not just the ceremony, but the whole journey. 🤍

We know this may not be what you imagined, and your feelings matter deeply to us. We are not asking you to agree right away — only to keep an open heart as we figure this out together. Whatever happens, you are loved, you are valued, and you will always be at the center of this story.
